Bio Metric

The Selection of a particular Biomatric for use in a specific application involves a specific application involves a weighing of several factors.In general the seven factors to be used accesses the suitability of any feature to use in biometric authentication.

Universality   means that every person using a system should possess the trait.

Uniqueness   means the attribute should be sufficiently different for individuals in the relevent population such that they can be distinguished from one another

Permanence   relates to the manner in which a feature varies over time.More Specifically, a quality with good permanence will be reasonably invarient over time with respect to the specific matching algorithm.

Measurability   relates to the ease of acquisition or measurement of the characterstics.In addition, acquired data should be in a form that permits subsequent processing and extraction of the relevent feature sets.

Performance   relates to the accuracy, speed and robustness of technology used.

Acceptability   relates to how well individuals in the relevent population accept the technology such that they are willing to have their biometric trait captured and assessed.

Circumvention   relates to the ease with which a peculiarity might be imitiated using an artifact or substitute.

A biomatric system can operate in the following two modes.

>>Verification Mode  
>>Identification Mode  

In Verification Mode   the system performs a one to one comparision of a captured biomatric with a specific template stored in a biometric database in order to verify the individual in the person they claim to be , such as smart card, username or ID Number.

In Identification Mode   the system performs a one to many comparision against a biometric database in attempt to establish the identify of an unknown individual through biometrics since other methods of personal recognisition such as passwords, PINs or keys are ineffective.
